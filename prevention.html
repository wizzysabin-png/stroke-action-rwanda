Prevention & Warning Signs

Stroke is a medical emergency — but many strokes can be prevented, and recovery is possible with timely care.

If you suspect a stroke, seek emergency medical care immediately. Every minute matters.

Understand the Warning Signs (F.A.S.T.)

Recognize stroke symptoms and know what to do immediately to save a life.

F

FACE

Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop?

A

ARMS

Ask them to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?

S

SPEECH

Ask them to repeat a simple phrase. Is their speech slurred?

T

TIME

If you see these signs, call for emergency help immediately.

How to Prevent Stroke

Lifestyle Changes

Maintain a healthy diet, exercise regularly, and avoid smoking to significantly reduce your risk.

Manage Health Conditions

Regular checkups to monitor blood pressure, cholesterol, and diabetes are essential prevention steps.

Know Your Risk

Understand how factors like age and family history contribute to your personal stroke risk.
